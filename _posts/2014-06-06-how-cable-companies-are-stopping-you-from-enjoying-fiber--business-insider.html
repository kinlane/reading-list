Everyone wants a piece of Google’s high-speed Fiber network , but the truth is many cities and communities already have their own fiber networks.As Motherboards Jason Koebler points out, they just cant use them.As you might guess, the giant telecom companies — including Time Warner Cable, Verizon, and Comcast — are involved here.These are the same companies trying to bully the FCC into creating an “internet fast lane” so web-based companies can pay them more for better service.For the most part, the cable companies have been offering incentives to local governments (mainly just better service) in exchange for noncompete clauses, which disallow those communities from adopting fiber networks that might rival their own services.
