Its standard to size a market by the number of widgets sold, but in the Internet of Things, which numbers sensors and devices in the billions, widget counts dont really matter.In part this is because the real money in IoT is not in the things, but rather in the Internet-enabled services that stitch them together.See also: The Internet Of Things: The Real Money Is In The Internet, Not The Things More to the point, its because the size of the IoT market fundamentally depends on the number of developers creating value in it. While today there are just 300,000 developers contributing to the IoT, a new report from VisionMobile projects a whopping 4.5 million developers by 2020, reflecting a 57% compound annual growth rate and a massive market opportunity.
