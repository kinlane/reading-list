Throughout my experience talking about and teaching platform strategy, I’ve been challenged to discover and codify the picture of how I see platforms working, especially in the context of consumer software built with a strong B2B backbone.While I don’t always conform to some traditional views— I try not to focus too hard on network effects— people familiar with and working within platforms will probably be accustomed.Let’s start with the definition of a platform, as many of us do.A platform is a suite of products and services that serves to create emergent ecosystem advantages through tradeoffs.These advantages may come in the form of users, revenue, enterprise value, optionality, and, especially, partnerships and M&A visibility.
