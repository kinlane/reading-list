Many of today’s global challenges are not new.Economic inequality, the unfettered power of corporations and markets, the need to cooperate to address global problems and the unsatisfactory levels of accountability in democratic governance – these were as much problems a century ago as they remain today.What has changed, however – and most markedly – is the role that new forms of information and information technology could potentially play in responding to these challenges.What’s going on?The incredible advances in digital technology mean we have an unprecedented ability to create, share and access information.
