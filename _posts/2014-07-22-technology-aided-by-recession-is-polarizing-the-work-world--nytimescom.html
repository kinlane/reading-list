The American work force has been growing polarized for decades.On one end, there are highly skilled jobs like writing software or performing surgery, and on the other are service jobs like child care and cutting hair.The jobs in the middle, meanwhile, such as factory work, sales and bookkeeping, are shrinking — one of the reasons for the economy’s slow climb out of the recession.Where did those jobs go?Part of the answer lies in Silicon Valley.
