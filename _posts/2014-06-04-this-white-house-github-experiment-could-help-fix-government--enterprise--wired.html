Illustration: Cameron McEfee/GitHub While many of our nation’s problems are quite clear, the way our government addresses them is too often a black box—opaque and closed to all but insiders and lobbyists.But the White House has taken a remarkable–if small–step toward bringing greater transparency to the legislative process.For the first time, it has used the GitHub social coding website as a forum for discussing and ultimately changing government policy.With one GitHub “pull request,” it modified the Project Open Data policy document, which spells out how government agencies are supposed to open up access to their data.This represents the fusion of open source software and government policy that open-government advocates have long predicted.
