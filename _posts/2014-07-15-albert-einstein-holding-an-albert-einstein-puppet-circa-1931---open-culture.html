The Yale Puppeteers, consisting of Forman Brown, Harry Burnett, and Roddy Brandon, came together in the 1920s and spent almost the next seven decades touring the United States, putting on satirical performances that featured puppets in starring roles.They also staged performances at the Turnabout Theater from 1941 to 1956, turning it into a Hollywood institution.Einstein saw the puppet perform at the Teatro Torito [a predecessor to the Turnabout Theater] and was quite amused.He reached into his jacket’s breast pocket, pulled out a letter and crumpled it up.Speaking in German, he said, ‘The puppet wasn’t fat enough!
